## What's Actually in Your Kratom Gummy?

A clean, quality kratom gummy should contain:

- **Kratom extract** — the active ingredient, clearly dosed
- **Pectin or gelatin** — the gelling agent (pectin = vegan)
- **Natural fruit juice or flavoring** — for taste
- **Sweetener** — sugar, cane syrup, or alternative sweeteners
- **Citric acid** — common preservative and flavor enhancer
- **Natural food coloring** — beet juice, turmeric, etc. (not artificial dyes)

**Watch out for:**
- Artificial dyes (Red 40, Yellow 5, etc.)
- Undisclosed botanical additions (kava, valerian, etc.)
- Excessive sugar content
- Preservatives beyond citric acid

The shorter and cleaner the ingredient list, the better.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do kratom gummies take to work?**
Expect 45–75 minutes for full effects — longer than powder or capsules due to the digestive process required to break down the gummy matrix. Be patient. Do not redose early.

**Are kratom gummies as effective as powder?**
At equivalent mitragynine doses, yes. The format affects onset timing and duration slightly but not the fundamental alkaloid effects. Quality matters more than format.

**Can I cut a kratom gummy in half for a smaller dose?**
Yes — if the gummy is uniformly dosed, cutting it provides a reliable half dose. This is a good strategy for new users starting low.

**Why do kratom gummies cost more than capsules or powder?**
Gummy production requires food-grade manufacturing, flavoring, sweeteners, gelling agents, and specialized equipment. The cost of goods is genuinely higher — not just a format premium.

**How should I store kratom gummies?**
Cool, dry location away from direct sunlight. Airtight container or resealable bag. Avoid refrigeration unless the label specifically recommends it — moisture from condensation can affect texture and potency. Shelf life is typically 12–18 months when stored correctly.

**Are kratom gummies vegan?**
Check the label. Pectin-based gummies are vegan. Gelatin-based gummies are not. Quality brands clearly state which gelling agent they use.
